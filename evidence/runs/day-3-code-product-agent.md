## Output

Client context:
Local renovation craftsperson requesting a showcase website to sell renovation services.

Problem:
The prospect needs a clearer web presence, but the requested scope is not yet defined enough for a final price or timeline.

Goal:
Prepare a small website or landing page that explains the renovation service, builds trust, and gives visitors a clear contact action.

Scope:
Initial scope hypothesis is a focused showcase website. The first version should stay small until services, proof, content readiness, and contact flow are confirmed.

Pages or deliverables:

- Home or landing page.
- Service overview section or page.
- Project proof section using real photos or examples if available.
- Contact form or contact call to action.
- Basic local SEO structure.

Assumptions:

- The craftsperson serves a local market.
- Renovation services can be explained through a small site.
- The client can provide photos, service details, and proof elements.

Open questions:

- Which renovation service should be promoted first?
- Which location or service area should the site target?
- Are photos, reviews, certifications, or past project examples available?
- Is copy already written, or should Web Studio OS draft it?
- Is a contact form required, or is phone/email enough?
- How many review rounds are expected?

Risks:

- Final timeline cannot be estimated without page count, content readiness, and review process.
- Final price cannot be quoted without validated scope.
- Missing proof assets may weaken the showcase value.
- Too many services in the first version may make the site unfocused.

Next action:
Run a short discovery checklist before creating any fictional quote or timeline range.
