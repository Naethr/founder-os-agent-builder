## Response Obtained

Based on the notes, this week you should learn the skills that reduce launch ambiguity fastest: offer clarity, discovery, quote framing, landing page basics, local SEO basics, sales drafting, and permission boundaries.

### What Is Explicitly Supported By The Notes

- Web Studio OS should stay focused on landing pages and small service websites for small service businesses. Source: `vault/10-business/business-brief.md`; Source: `vault/30-offers/web-studio-offer.md`
- The MVP offer includes positioning, service copy, basic local SEO structure, lead capture, and maintenance process basics. Source: `vault/30-offers/web-studio-offer.md`
- The first client profiles to test include local service businesses, freelancers, artisans, coaches, consultants, and small business owners. Source: `vault/20-clients/target-clients.md`
- Sales communication should ask clarifying questions before giving a price or timeline. Source: `vault/50-sales-mail/sales-tone.md`; Source: `vault/60-admin/admin-rules.md`
- SEO work should stay basic and honest: service-specific pages, clear headings, page titles, meta descriptions, local modifiers only when true, and no guaranteed SEO outcomes. Source: `vault/40-seo-market/seo-ideas.md`
- External, financial, public, permanent, and privacy-sensitive actions require human validation. Source: `vault/60-admin/permissions.md`; Source: `vault/60-admin/admin-rules.md`

### Recommended One-Week Learning Plan

Day 1: Refine the offer and scope.

- Rewrite the Web Studio OS offer in one paragraph.
- Define what is included and excluded from the MVP.
- Practice explaining why the first version is a landing page or small website, not a broad agency platform.
- Sources: `vault/30-offers/web-studio-offer.md`; `vault/10-business/decisions.md`

Day 2: Learn discovery questions.

- Practice the questions needed before quoting: service sold, ideal customer, proof, visitor action, content readiness, and exclusions.
- Use one fictional local service business to test the questions.
- Sources: `vault/50-sales-mail/sales-tone.md`; `vault/20-clients/target-clients.md`

Day 3: Learn quote framing.

- Separate setup work from monthly maintenance.
- Write a quote outline that lists assumptions, review gates, content needs, and scope limits.
- Do not treat the documented pricing ranges as validated market prices.
- Sources: `vault/60-admin/admin-rules.md`; `vault/30-offers/web-studio-offer.md`

Day 4: Learn landing page conversion basics.

- Study the minimum page structure: clear headline, service promise, proof, objections, call to action, and lead capture.
- Draft a simple structure for one service page.
- Sources: `vault/30-offers/web-studio-offer.md`; `vault/70-learning/learning-plan.md`

Day 5: Learn local SEO basics.

- Practice writing page titles, meta descriptions, headings, and internal link ideas for one service-specific page.
- Keep the claims modest and do not promise rankings.
- Sources: `vault/40-seo-market/seo-ideas.md`; `vault/70-learning/learning-plan.md`

Day 6: Practice a sales email draft.

- Write one short reply that asks for missing scope before pricing.
- Keep the tone direct, specific, and practical.
- Do not invent testimonials, deadlines, discounts, or client facts.
- Sources: `vault/50-sales-mail/sales-tone.md`; `vault/60-admin/permissions.md`

Day 7: Review permission and validation boundaries.

- Check whether any draft would send an email, publish content, quote a final price, store personal data, or update official decisions.
- Mark those actions as requiring human validation.
- Sources: `vault/60-admin/permissions.md`; `vault/60-admin/admin-rules.md`
