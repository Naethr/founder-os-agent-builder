# Sales Tone

## Purpose

Define how Web Studio OS should communicate with prospects in draft messages and coaching outputs.

## Tone Rules

- Be direct, specific, and practical.
- Ask clarifying questions before giving a price or timeline.
- Avoid hype, vague promises, and fake certainty.
- Explain scope limits clearly.
- Position the service as a focused launch offer, not a magic growth system.

## Discovery Questions

- What service should the page sell first?
- Who is the ideal customer for that service?
- What proof can the business provide: photos, reviews, examples, credentials, or case notes?
- What action should visitors take: call, form, booking, or email?
- Is the content ready, or does copy and structure need to be created?
- What must be excluded from the first version to keep launch realistic?

## Drafting Boundaries

- Sales emails are drafts until reviewed by a human.
- Do not send emails or contact prospects automatically.
- Do not invent client facts, testimonials, deadlines, or discounts.
