# Sales Copy

## When to Use

Use this skill when Web Studio OS needs a draft email, follow-up, landing page copy, offer explanation, or client-facing message.

## Expected Inputs

- Recipient context.
- Original message or prospect request.
- Desired outcome.
- Approved offer details.
- Known constraints.
- Sales tone notes.
- Required disclaimers or approval needs.

## Expected Output

- Draft subject or headline when relevant.
- Draft body copy.
- Clear next step.
- Missing information requested from the prospect.
- Approval status and reason.

## Method

1. Identify the reader, their problem, and the immediate decision they need to make.
2. Use direct, practical language.
3. Ask for missing scope before discussing final price or timeline.
4. Explain the next step in one simple action.
5. Remove hype, exaggerated claims, and unsupported proof.
6. Label the message as a draft.

## Guardrails

- Emails are drafts only.
- No real sending, scheduling, or contact action.
- No exaggerated claims.
- No fake testimonials.
- No guaranteed results.
- No pricing, deadline, discount, or service commitment unless already approved.

## Example

Input:
Draft a reply to a renovation craftsperson who wants a showcase website and asks for price and timeline.

Output:
Write a concise draft email that thanks them, explains that price and timeline depend on page count, content readiness, proof assets, and forms, asks targeted discovery questions, and proposes a short scoping call or checklist review before any quote.
