# Prospecting

## When to Use

Use this skill when Web Studio OS needs target segments, lead hypotheses, qualification criteria, or outreach preparation for small service businesses.

## Expected Inputs

- Target market or segment.
- Geographic scope if provided.
- Current offer.
- Qualification criteria.
- Approved data sources.
- Permission constraints.

## Expected Output

- Target segment hypotheses.
- Lead fit criteria.
- Pain hypotheses.
- Personalization angles.
- Outreach preparation checklist.
- Approval requirements before storing data or contacting anyone.

## Method

1. Start from approved target client profiles and offer scope.
2. Define why the segment might have a real website or sales problem.
3. Identify qualification signals and disqualification signals.
4. Prepare outreach angles without contacting anyone.
5. Minimize personal data.
6. State what requires human approval before any external action.

## Guardrails

- No scraping.
- No importing personal data without approval.
- No automated outreach.
- Comply with data minimization.
- Do not enrich leads with paid tools unless approved.
- Do not claim real market validation unless evidence exists.

## Example

Input:
Prepare prospecting hypotheses for local renovation contractors.

Output:
List target subsegments, fit signals such as service clarity and proof assets, pain hypotheses around trust and lead capture, safe outreach preparation steps, and explicit approval requirements before storing contacts or sending messages.
